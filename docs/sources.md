## Methodology Notes

### Ownership Data Assembly
Ownership data for Yakutia is **fragmented across multiple sources** and was consolidated manually using:
1. Public corporate disclosures (annual reports, MOEX filings)
2. Government procurement and property databases
3. Industry press (Kommersant, Vedomosti, RBC — business press)

### Simplification of Ownership Categories
Complex beneficial ownership chains were simplified into five categories:

| Category | Definition |
|----------|------------|
| **Federal** | Russian Federation government, federal state enterprises, federally-controlled corporations (Rosneft, RusHydro, Gazprom, RZhD) |
| **Regional** | Republic of Sakha (Yakutia) government, republic-controlled enterprises |
| **Municipal** | Municipal district governments within Yakutia |
| **Private** | Privately-held Russian corporations, oligarch-controlled entities, public float |
| **Foreign** | Non-Russian entities (Indian consortium, international investors) |

### Revenue Estimates
- Revenue figures represent **estimated annual revenue attributable to Yakutia operations** (not group-wide)
- Sources: company annual reports, analyst estimates, Rosstat sectoral data
- Figures in USD millions; converted at approximate exchange rates for the reference period (2021–2022 pre-sanction benchmark)
- Where exact Yakutia-specific revenue is unavailable, proportional estimates are used based on production share

### Key Limitations
1. **Beneficial ownership opacity**: Several private entities (Surgutneftegas) have deliberately opaque ownership; the registered corporate owner is used
2. **Sanctions impact**: Post-February 2022 ownership structures (particularly foreign stakes) are in flux; pre-2022 structure is used as the analytical baseline
3. **Almazy Anabara**: Treated as ALROSA subsidiary (private category) though ALROSA itself has mixed federal/regional/private ownership
4. **Revenue attribution**: Allocating group revenues to Yakutia operations involves estimation; figures should be treated as indicative rather than precise
5. **Elga Coal Complex**: One of the world's largest undeveloped coking coal deposits; revenue figures reflect early-stage production (2022–2023 ramp-up), not full potential
